First-Thing-First

Hello world, my name is Tanner Phan. I’m a software developer based in Seattle, WA. In college, I wanted to study Computer Engineering, but instead, I had a mix of Physics, Math, and Computer Software before dropping out and worked on a start-up with a group of awesome people.

The truth is I was unhappy with the learning experience, and I was getting tired of hoops I needed to jump through in order to learn to write software. I was determined to learn how to write software, and later on, I even found that I had the aptitude for Machine Learning. (Shout out Udacity!)

The people I started my first start-up with are now my life-long friends. Unfortunately, the start-up didn’t survive the market test. Being the only developer, I was tough for me to make the decision to stop the development process. As the saying goes “fail early, fail graciously”. Ultimately we came to the conclusion that the future is not where we thought, and celebrated our journey to that point. The site was served more than ten thousand times, to over a thousand people, when it was taken down.

I was not going to let no school telling me what I skill I can and can not learn. And I think this is important. You need a resilient mentality to survive the journey of becoming a software developer. For me, understanding why I want to learn to write software so badly helped me get through difficult times. My reason for learning how to write software stems from my desire to contribute to building a better world of tomorrow.

This blog will mostly be about writing software but I will occasionally talk about entrepreneurial related topics.

So, first thing first. What makes you want to write software more than breathing?
