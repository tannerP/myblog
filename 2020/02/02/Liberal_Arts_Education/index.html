Liberal Arts Education and Empathy

Tanner Phan
02-Feb-2020

After taking a couple of years off, I got a chance to return to university and majoring in Computer Science at Seattle Pacific University. I’m enjoying my time here, but I do need to move on and graduate. The liberal arts education at SPU has enriched my life. For many years, my focus was on the technical side of software building and trying to answer these questions: How to build a full-stack web application? How to scale it to millions of users? What tools or frameworks to use for the job?

I’ve come to value nontechnical knowledge as much as I do with technical knowledge. I think having some understanding about Philosophy, and History of Modern Global Systems are useful in product ideation. So far, they’ve helped me to better understand human nature and show more appreciation to the progress we’ve made thus far as a collective society. In terms of product development, I feel that I’m more abled to empathize with users and their problems, to come up with holistic product solutions.

I feel grateful to have had the opportunity to complete my B.S. from SPU. It enabled me to understand myself and others better. I’m not sure what lies ahead once I graduate, but I’m already feeling like missing this place.
