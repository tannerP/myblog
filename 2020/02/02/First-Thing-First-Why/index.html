Liberal Arts Education for Better User Experience

Tanner Phan
02-Feb-2020

After taking a couple of years off, I got a chance to return to university and majoring in Computer Science at Seattle Pacific University. I’m really enjoying my time here, but I do need to move on and graduate. The liberal arts education at SPU has really enriched my life. For many years, my focus was on the technical side of creating software: How to build a full-stack software application? How to scale it to millions of users? What tools or frameworks to use?

Recently I’ve come to realize that the nontechnical aspects are just as important. Areas like Philosohpy and Social Science are important in ideation and answer the Why – “Why are we spending our time building this?”. This kinds of knowledge further helps to empathize with users, in order to build a compelling piece of software.

This is why I’m so grateful for my education from SPU; it enabled me to understand other people better. I’m not sure what lies ahead once I graduate, but I’m already feeling like missing this place.
