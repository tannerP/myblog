Building Studimore

Tanner Phan
31-Dec-2020

Building Studimore for the kids! I was fortunate in 2015 to have earned a software internship and, after that, a contract to work at Distelli. I’m grateful to those who opened the door for me, and I hope to do the same thing for the next groups of students. With Studimore, we’re out to give low-income family students early access to fulfilling and high paying careers, giving them opportunities and something direct their youthful energy.

In 2015, being the oldest sibling, I decided to move back home and stayed with my mother and youngest sister. With my flexible work schedule, I could give my youngest sister rides to school and attend her extracurricular activities. Working from home was challenging at first, but eventually, I learned to deal with my career progression uncertainties and lead a fulfilling life.

Babysitting was not new to me then. Though the first time, I did a pretty bad job with my middle sister. Our parents left her and me to my grandfather when I was nine years old. I became both Mom and Dad immediately. My sister wasn’t shy to let me know I sucked at parenting when we became teenagers. This was a chance for me to do better. I would travel to Vietnam in the Summer when my youngest sister, Tiffany, was out of school. When I came back from Vietnam and hearing, Tiffany complained to me about what a miserable summer she’s had gave me a sense of accomplishment. I’m also glad I made it easier for her by moving back home for a few years.

I’ll finish my essay with this quote from Paul Grahm’s essay:

“If life seems awful to kids, it’s neither because hormones are turning you all into monsters (as your parents believe), nor because life actually is awful (as you believe). It’s because the adults, who no longer have any economic use for you, have abandoned you to spend years cooped up together with nothing real to do. Any society of that type is awful to live in. You don’t have to look any further to explain why teenage kids are unhappy.”
